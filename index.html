Cross‑Sectional 12‑1 Momentum

Does a stock's past 12‑month return, skipping the most recent month, predict its rank return over the following month? A reproducible S&P 500 backtest — from raw prices to net‑of‑cost, market‑neutral performance.

Signal — 12‑1 momentum
MOMi,t = Pi,t−21 / Pi,t−252 − 1
Return from ~12 months ago to ~1 month ago. The 1‑month skip avoids short‑term reversal. Composited with a 6‑1 term, cross‑sectionally z‑scored each month.
Portfolio construction
Universe rebuilt monthly: S&P 500 members, top 100 by 21‑day average dollar volume, price ≥ $5, ≥ 1yr history. Rank into quintiles; long Q5, short Q1, equal‑weighted, monthly rebalance. Costs modeled at 10 bps per side.
The honest read: the top quintile compounds impressively, but much of that is simply equity‑market exposure. The real test is the market‑neutral long‑short, which earns a modest, cost‑sensitive premium — and the monthly information coefficient is positive but weak. This is a genuine, un‑oversold result.

Method & honest limitations

What this does — and doesn't — prove

Data is pulled from Yahoo Finance and cleaned (OHLC sanity checks, non‑positive prices, zero‑volume flats). Signals and forward returns are shifted strictly within each ticker to avoid look‑ahead. Cross‑sectional z‑scores are winsorized at the 1st/99th percentile before ranking. Standard errors on the predictive regression are clustered by date, which pulls the naïve t‑stat of 3.8 down to a more honest 1.6.

  • Survivorship bias. The universe is built from today's S&P 500 membership, so delisted and dropped names are absent — this flatters the backtest.
  • Close‑to‑close returns. No open/VWAP execution modeling; real fills would differ.
  • Constant linear costs. 10 bps/side is a simplification — no market impact, borrow cost, or spread dynamics.
  • No risk model. Buckets are not sector‑ or beta‑neutralized in the headline run, so the Q5 basket carries obvious factor tilts (it is currently ~90% technology).
