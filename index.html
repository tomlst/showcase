Cross-Sectional 12-1 Momentum in U.S. Large Caps

Shaotong (Tom) Li  ·   · 

Abstract

I test whether a stock's past 12-month return, excluding the most recent month, predicts its cross-sectional return over the following month within a liquid S&P 500 universe. Ranking monthly into quintiles, the top quintile earns a Sharpe of 1.20 gross, but a market-neutral long–short of the extremes retains only a modest, cost-sensitive premium; the monthly rank information coefficient is positive but weak. Results are presented gross and net of a simple transaction-cost model, with limitations stated plainly.

1. Data and signal construction

Daily prices are retrieved from Yahoo Finance and cleaned for OHLC consistency, non-positive prices, and zero-volume flats. All signals and forward returns are shifted strictly within each ticker to preclude look-ahead.

3. Limitations

The predictive regression's naïve t-statistic of 3.8 falls to 1.6 once standard errors are clustered by date — the honest significance of the signal. Four further caveats bound the interpretation:

  • Survivorship bias. The universe uses current S&P 500 membership, so delisted names are absent, flattering the backtest.
  • Close-to-close returns. No open/VWAP execution modeling; realized fills would differ.
  • Constant linear costs. Ten basis points per side omits market impact, borrow, and spread dynamics.
  • No risk model. Buckets are not sector- or beta-neutralized, so the Q5 basket carries pronounced factor tilts (presently ~90% technology).
